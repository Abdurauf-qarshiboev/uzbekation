FAQs

What regions of Uzbekistan does Uzbekation cover?

Uzbekation offers tours to all regions of Uzbekistan including Tashkent, Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva, Fergana Valley, and the Aral Sea. Our specialized guides ensure you experience both popular destinations and hidden gems throughout the country.

What is the best time of year to visit Uzbekistan?

While Uzbekation operates year-round, spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer the most pleasant weather for sightseeing. Summer (June-August) can be very hot, especially in desert regions, while winter (November-March) brings cooler temperatures but fewer crowds at major attractions.

Do I need a visa to visit Uzbekistan?

Many countries now enjoy visa-free entry to Uzbekistan for up to 30 days. Our team will advise you on visa requirements based on your nationality and can assist with the e-visa application process if needed. We recommend checking visa requirements at least 1-2 months before your planned trip.

How long do your typical tours last?

Uzbekation offers tours ranging from 3-day city experiences to comprehensive 14-day journeys across the country. Our most popular "Silk Road Highlights" package lasts 7-8 days, covering the historic cities of Tashkent, Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva. We also create custom itineraries based on your preferences and available time.

What languages do your guides speak?

All Uzbekation guides are fluent in English. We also have guides available who speak Russian, French, German, Spanish, Italian, Chinese, Japanese, and Korean. Please request your preferred language when booking, and we'll match you with an appropriate guide.

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancellations made 30+ days before departure receive a full refund minus a $50 administration fee. Cancellations 15-29 days before departure receive a 75% refund. Cancellations 7-14 days before departure receive a 50% refund. Unfortunately, we cannot offer refunds for cancellations less than 7 days before departure. Travel insurance is highly recommended.

Is Uzbekistan safe for tourists?

Uzbekistan is considered one of the safest countries in Central Asia for tourists. Crime rates are low, and locals are typically very hospitable toward visitors. As with travel anywhere, basic precautions should be taken. Our guides will advise on local customs and appropriate behavior to ensure you have a comfortable and secure experience.

What accommodations do you offer?

Uzbekation provides accommodations ranging from luxury 5-star hotels to authentic boutique guesthouses in restored historic buildings. For adventurous travelers, we also offer yurt stays in desert regions. Our standard packages include 3-4 star hotels, but accommodations can be upgraded or modified based on your preferences and budget.

Do you offer group discounts?

Yes! Groups of 6-10 people receive a 10% discount on all tour packages. Groups of 11+ receive a 15% discount and a complimentary traditional Uzbek dinner show. We specialize in organizing tours for families, friends, corporate groups, and special interest clubs. Contact us for custom group package options.
