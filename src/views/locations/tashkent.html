Tashkent

Modern Capital

Tashkent, the capital and largest city of Uzbekistan, blends Soviet-era architecture with modern developments and historic Islamic influences. After an earthquake in 1966 destroyed much of the city, it was rebuilt with wide avenues, spacious parks, and monuments. Despite the modernization, Tashkent still preserves parts of its old town, with traditional houses and religious buildings. The city serves as the cultural and economic heart of the country, featuring numerous museums, theaters, and educational institutions.

Transportation

Tashkent has Central Asia's oldest metro system, with stations that are works of art showcasing Uzbek history.

Urban life

Experience both tradition and modernity with vibrant bazaars, restaurants, and cafes and tea houses.

Culture

Home to world-class museums including the State Museum of History of Uzbekistan and Museum of Applied Arts.

Weather info:

Tashkent has a continental climate with hot summers and cold winters. Summer temperatures typically reach 35°C in July and August, while winter brings occasional snow and temperatures around 0°C.
