Bukhara

Historic Center

The historic center of Bukhara, a city on the Silk Road, is more than 2,000 years old. It is one of the best examples of well-preserved Islamic cities of Central Asia, with over 140 architectural monuments dating from the 9th to the 19th centuries. UNESCO included the entire historic center of Bukhara in the World Heritage List in 1993. The city's urban fabric remains largely intact, showcasing centuries of history through its madrasas, mosques, minarets, and domed bazaars.

Where to sleep

Historic hotels and traditional guesthouses inside ancient merchant houses provide authentic experiences.

Local cuisine

Try Bukharian specialties like shashlik, plov, and shurpa in centuries-old restaurants throughout the city.

Shopping

Famous for its carpet weavingnand traditional crafts sold in historic trading domes and bazaars.

Weather info:

Bukhara has a desert climate with hot, dry summers and cold winters. Summer temperatures regularly exceed 40°C in July with low humidity, making spring and autumn the most comfortable seasons to visit.
