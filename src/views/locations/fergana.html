Fergana Valley

Silk & Orchards

The Fergana Valley is one of the most fertile regions in Central Asia, surrounded by the Tian Shan and Pamir mountain ranges. Historically a center of the Silk Road, it remains Uzbekistan's agricultural heartland, producing fruits, vegetables, cotton, and silk. The region is home to several ancient cities including Andijan, Fergana, and Namangan, each with distinct cultural traditions. The valley is famous for its traditional silk production, ceramics, and woodworking, with artisanal techniques passed down through generations.

Silk Heritage

Visit traditional silk workshops where artisans still create vibrant fabrics using ancient techniques.

Ceramics

Rishtan pottery, with distinctive blue glaze, is produced using techniques dating back centuries.

Orchards

The valley's fertile soil produces some of Central Asia's most delicious fruits, especially melons, apricots, and pomegranates.

Weather info:

The Fergana Valley enjoys a continental climate with hot, dry summers and moderately cold winters. Protected by mountains, the valley experiences less temperature extremes than other parts of Uzbekistan, with average summer temperatures around 30°C.
